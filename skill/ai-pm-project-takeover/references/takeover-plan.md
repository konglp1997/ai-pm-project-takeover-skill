# Prioritization and 30/60/90-day takeover plan

Turn understanding into a sequenced operating plan.

## Prioritization model

Score candidates with:

- User/business impact.
- Risk reduction.
- Decision urgency.
- Evidence confidence.
- Effort and dependency.
- Reversibility.

Do not collapse everything into one numeric score. Present trade-offs and confidence.

Use four buckets:

1. **Safeguard now:** security, privacy, harmful action, severe reliability, misleading claims.
2. **Clarify before committing:** E0/E1 assumptions that block roadmap or launch decisions.
3. **Quick win:** bounded change with evidence and measurable outcome.
4. **Strategic bet:** larger hypothesis requiring discovery, evaluation, or experiment.

## 30 days: establish truth and control

Typical outcomes:

- Canonical product thesis, user model, journey, scope, and rollout map.
- AI capability and dependency map.
- Metric/evaluation baseline and instrumentation gaps.
- Top risks, incidents, manual operations, and product debt.
- Stakeholder map, cadence, and decision log.

Exit criteria must be observable, such as “owners approve capability rollout states” rather than “understand the project.”

## 60 days: validate and improve

Typical outcomes:

- Resolve highest-impact unknowns with research, instrumentation, or experiments.
- Establish representative eval set and release gates.
- Ship one or two evidence-backed quick wins.
- Reduce a major reliability, cost, latency, safety, or workflow risk.
- Align roadmap around outcomes, constraints, and dependencies.

## 90 days: establish operating system

Typical outcomes:

- Repeatable product/AI review cadence.
- Trusted scorecard connecting business, product, AI, safety, and system metrics.
- Prioritized roadmap with explicit hypotheses and evaluation plans.
- Documented ownership, escalation, incident learning, and model/prompt change process.
- Executive narrative and investment asks.

## Plan format

| Horizon | Outcome | Actions | Evidence | Dependencies | Owner | Exit criteria |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Keep actions tied to decisions. Avoid a generic activity list.

## Stakeholder validation agenda

Tailor questions:

- Leadership: strategy, business model, constraints, non-negotiables.
- Product/design: users, evidence, journeys, roadmap decisions, UX trust.
- Engineering/ML: runtime truth, limitations, evals, debt, operations.
- Data: definitions, instrumentation, lineage, access, quality.
- Legal/security: data terms, risk classification, incidents, approvals.
- Sales/success/support: objections, adoption blockers, manual work, failure impact.
- Operations/reviewers: hidden labor, escalation, quality calibration.

Ask for examples and evidence, not opinions alone. Record owner and follow-up date.
